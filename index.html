About Me


Ever since I was young, I had a keen interest in healthcare. Growing up, my dream was actually to become a doctor, but that was before I got exposed to technology at MIT. I started off as a pre-med student, but I soon learned I was at a unique intersection between healthcare and technology, with the potential to help improve care at scale.

After graduating from MIT, I found the right opportunity as a Sales Engineer at InterSystems that put me at the forefront of healthcare and technology. Working with large provider organizations in the United States (i.e. Ascension Health, Intermountain Healthcare, Cedars-Sinai Medical Center), I solved challenging problems around healthcare data interoperability and integration, making it possible to aggregate data from various systems across hospitals (i.e. Epic/Cerner, labs, claims) and extracting actionable insights to deliver optimized care. To help a provider organization save on expensive nursing resources, I implemented an Alexa skill leveraging voice user interface (VUI) to routinely check a patient’s pain level, run machine learning to predict patient’s future pain level for pain management, and integrate into clinical workflows to send real-time alerts to clinicians. Also, with CMS-9115 on Interoperability and Patient Access, there has been a massive push towards FHIR in healthcare, and I helped customers become FHIR-enabled with reduced ETL complexity on InterSystems platform.

The more I saw the challenge with gaining value from the massive amounts of data sitting idle at hospitals, I wanted to make an impact on how healthcare organizations can leverage the data to deliver optimized care for patients. Earlier in 2021, I transitioned to become a Product Manager on the Healthcare Analytics team ("Health Insight") at InterSystems.

Outside of work, I like to stay active, whether it be running a 5K, coding new projects, and trying out new eateries to share on my food blog @EatLikeABOS. I am also a huge basketball fan, always on the lookout for the next Woj bomb in the NBA.
