About Me


I have always had a keen interest in healthcare. Growing up, I had my eyes set on medical school. However, once I came to MIT, I realized I was at a unique intersection between healthcare and technology.

After MIT, I knew I had to be out in the field and talk with customers to figure out what's the current landscape of digital health. I started off as a Sales Engineer at InterSystems, the technology powering Epic, that put me at the forefront of healthcare and technology. Most importantly, on a daily basis, I was talking with physicians and enterprise IT teams at some of the largest and innovative healthcare organizations in the United States, like Ascension Health, Intermountain, Northwell, and Cedars-Sinai. I solved challenging problems around healthcare data integration and interoperability (i.e. HL7, CDA, X12, FHIR), enabling data consolidation from different systems across hospitals (i.e. Epic/Cerner, labs, claims, SDOH) and extracting actionable insights to improve care.

Over the years, I saw precious healthcare data siloed at hospitals across different vendor software and the misaligned incentives that prevent effective utilization of patient data to improve care. Earlier this year, I returned to MIT in pursuit of entrepreneurship in healthcare to find an optimal way to help improve care using data, including exploring new enabling technologies like Web3.

I like to stay active, whether it be running a 5K, attending conferences, and trying out new eateries to share on my food blog @EatLikeABOS. I am also a huge basketball fan, always on the lookout for the next Woj bomb in the NBA.
