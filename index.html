About Me


I am the founder of a healthcare tech startup using AI to optimize clinical trials.

I have always had a keen interest in medicine growing up. I was a pre-med during my undergrad years at MIT, but given my passion for technology, I also studied Computer Science. Once I realized I was at a unique intersection between healthcare and technology, I wanted to help revolutionize healthcare with cutting-edge technology.

I am all about listening to the customer and seeing what's really going out in the real world. To gain those insights, I started off as a Sales Engineer in Healthcare at InterSystems, the platform that powers Epic electronic health record systems. I worked closely with engineers and executives at health systems, payers, and pharma to leverage patient data from data siloes to extract insights that can help improve care. After several years, I became a Product Manager leading the healthcare analytics team.

To innovate more rapidly and get closer to customer problems, I decided to start my own company. Coming back to MIT for grad school was a great opportunity to take advantage of entrepreneurship resources at MIT, ranging from Sloan, Martin Trust Center, iHQ, and even Harvard Innovation Labs.

I like to stay active in various dimensions of life, whether it be reading, lifting weights, traveling to new places, and uncovering new eateries to share on my food blog @EatLikeABOS. I am also a huge basketball fan, always on the lookout for the next Woj bomb in the NBA. Go Clippers! (yes, I know...)

Experiences


InterSystems

I started my career as a Sales Engineer and most recently was the Product Manager of the Healthcare Analytics team. I was focused on the analytics end user personas at health systems, not only solving data integration and interoperability challenges, but also extracting actionable insights to improve care for patients.

Massachusetts Institute of Technology

I completed the Master of Engineering program focused on AI. As an undergrad, I studied Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (Course 6) and minored in Biology (Course 7). I served as the President of Korean Students Association (KSA), competed nationally with the MIT Archery Club, and volunteered with Camp Kesem at MIT.

United States Archery Team

I picked up a bow and arrows in 2010 and was fortunate to train under USA National Head Coach Kisik Lee. I shoot an Olympic-style recurve, and I competed nationally and internationally over the years, making a team medal match appearance at the 2014 International Archery Festa. I earned selections to the 2014 US Archery Team and USA Junior Dream Team.
